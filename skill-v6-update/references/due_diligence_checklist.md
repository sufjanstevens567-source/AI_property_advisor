# Due Diligence Checklist

Use this checklist for each property before committing to purchase. Items marked [CRITICAL] must be resolved before proceeding. Items marked [IMPORTANT] should be resolved but may not be deal-breakers depending on findings.

---

## Legal & Title

- [ ] [CRITICAL] Solicitor engaged and title search completed
- [ ] [CRITICAL] Confirm clean title with no encumbrances, liens, or disputes
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Check for any pending litigation involving the property or development
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Verify planning compliance for the unit and building
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Check if any compulsory purchase orders or infrastructure plans affect area

## Rent Control & Tenancy

- [ ] [CRITICAL] Confirm RPZ (Rent Pressure Zone) status of the area
- [ ] [CRITICAL] Establish previous rent registered with RTB (if any tenancy existed)
- [ ] [CRITICAL] If existing tenant: confirm current rent, lease terms, Part 4 rights
- [ ] [CRITICAL] If previous tenant: confirm rent legally achievable on re-let under RPZ rules
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Check RTB register for any disputes involving this property
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Confirm vacant possession at completion (if buying vacant)
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Review any existing lease/tenancy agreement for unusual terms

## Building & OMC

- [ ] [CRITICAL] Obtain last 3 years of OMC accounts and AGM minutes
- [ ] [CRITICAL] Check for any special levies — past, present, or planned
- [ ] [CRITICAL] Confirm no outstanding fire-safety, water-ingress, or structural defect remediation
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Review sinking fund balance and adequacy
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Check OMC insurance policy — is building adequately insured?
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Identify OMC management agent; check reputation
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Ask solicitor about any Multi-Unit Developments Act compliance issues
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Check if building has a valid Fire Safety Certificate
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Ask about any Latent Defects Insurance (for newer buildings)

## Physical Condition

- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Commission independent survey / snag list (especially Celtic Tiger builds)
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Check for damp, mould, water ingress signs during viewing
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Verify BER certificate is current and accurate
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Assess condition of: windows, heating system, bathroom, kitchen, flooring
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Check common areas: lobby, lifts, stairwells, parking, bin areas
- [ ] Assess furnishing/setup costs required before letting

## Financial & Mortgage

- [ ] [CRITICAL] Mortgage approval in principle obtained (if using leverage)
- [ ] [CRITICAL] Confirm lender is comfortable with: LTV, property type, non-resident status
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Confirm exact mortgage rate and product available at current LTV
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Budget stamp duty (1% up to €1M)
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Budget legal fees, valuation, and setup costs
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Confirm insurance requirements from lender
- [ ] Confirm no issues with property type/age/area for lending

## Tax & Compliance

- [ ] [CRITICAL] Engage Irish tax advisor re: non-resident landlord obligations
- [ ] [CRITICAL] Appoint Irish collecting agent (required for non-resident landlords) OR confirm withholding approach
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Register with Revenue as Irish landlord
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Understand interaction with Bulgarian tax obligations (DTA)
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Register property with RTB within 1 month of tenancy start
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Confirm approach to mortgage interest deductibility (100% for rental)
- [ ] Understand pre-letting expense rules if property needs work before first let

## Rental Market Validation

- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Verify rent assumption with current Daft.ie comparables (not just asking rents — check actual lets if possible)
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Check same-building rental listings for evidence
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Speak to local letting agents about achievable rent and demand
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Understand seasonal letting patterns for the area
- [ ] Consider tenant profile: who rents here? Professionals, students, families?

## Insurance

- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Obtain landlord insurance quote (building + contents + liability)
- [ ] [IMPORTANT] Check if OMC block policy covers building (often does — you need contents/liability only)
- [ ] Confirm rent protection insurance availability and cost

## Operational Setup

- [ ] Decide: self-manage or appoint managing agent
- [ ] If self-managing from abroad: plan for key-holding, emergency access, maintenance contact
- [ ] Budget for initial furnishing (if letting furnished)
- [ ] Prepare standard tenancy agreement (use RTB template as base)
- [ ] Plan approach to tenant screening and referencing

---

## Post-Purchase (First 30 Days)

- [ ] Register with RTB
- [ ] Set up Irish bank account (if not already in place)
- [ ] Confirm tax agent/accountant appointment
- [ ] Set up utility transfers / confirm arrangements with OMC
- [ ] Confirm insurance is active
- [ ] Begin marketing (if vacant) or confirm tenant relationship (if occupied)
