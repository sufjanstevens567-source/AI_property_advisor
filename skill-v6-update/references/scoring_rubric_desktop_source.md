# Qualitative Scoring Rubric

Score each property 1–10 on each dimension. Use the anchors below for consistency.

---

## 1. Location Quality

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | D2/D4 premium, Ballsbridge, Merrion, St Stephen's Green, top Grand Canal Dock |
| 7–8 | Good D2/D4/D6, Smithfield premium, strong southside, Ringsend improving |
| 5–6 | Decent but not premium: established northside pockets, IFSC fringe, solid suburbs |
| 3–4 | Mixed areas, transitional, some negative perception, limited amenity |
| 1–2 | Known problem areas, isolated, very weak amenity, stigmatized |

## 2. Safety / Tenant Perception

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Perceived as very safe; professionals/families comfortable walking at night |
| 7–8 | Generally safe; occasional minor incidents but strong overall perception |
| 5–6 | Mixed perception; some tenants would hesitate, others fine |
| 3–4 | Noticeable safety concerns raised in forums/Reddit; some avoidance |
| 1–2 | Widely perceived as unsafe; significant tenant reluctance |

## 3. Recession Resilience

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Demand holds even in downturns; large employer base nearby; essential-worker proximity |
| 7–8 | Demand likely to dip moderately but recover quickly; diverse tenant pool |
| 5–6 | Would see noticeable vacancy/rent pressure in a downturn |
| 3–4 | Highly cyclical; dependent on a narrow employment sector or transient demand |
| 1–2 | First to suffer vacancy; last to recover; very narrow demand base |

## 4. Tenant Demand

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Would let within days; massive demand pool; waiting lists common |
| 7–8 | Strong demand; lets within 1–2 weeks at market rent |
| 5–6 | Adequate demand; may take 2–4 weeks; some competition from similar stock |
| 3–4 | Softer demand; may need rent concession or longer void |
| 1–2 | Difficult to let; very narrow or weak tenant pool |

## 5. Likelihood of Achieving Base Rent

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Multiple same-building comps confirm base rent; high confidence |
| 7–8 | Strong area comps; minor uncertainty but base rent is realistic |
| 5–6 | Reasonable estimate but limited direct evidence; could be ±5–10% |
| 3–4 | Thin evidence; significant uncertainty; rent may be optimistic |
| 1–2 | No reliable comps; rent assumption is speculative |

## 6. Rent-Control / Legal Rent-Setting Confidence

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Vacant possession, no RPZ constraint, or new-to-market with clear legal basis for market rent |
| 7–8 | RPZ applies but previous rent is close to market; modest reset risk |
| 5–6 | Some uncertainty about previous rent or RPZ compliance; needs verification |
| 3–4 | Previous rent materially below market; RPZ reset rules may cap achievable rent |
| 1–2 | Clear legal barrier to charging market rent; or major unresolved dispute |

## 7. Building Quality

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Modern, well-maintained, no known defects, strong construction quality |
| 7–8 | Good condition; minor cosmetic issues only; well-managed building |
| 5–6 | Adequate but aging; some maintenance backlog; no structural concerns |
| 3–4 | Visible issues; reports of defects or remediation needed; Celtic Tiger concerns |
| 1–2 | Known serious defects; fire safety, water ingress, structural issues; remediation ongoing/needed |

## 8. OMC / Service-Charge / Remediation Risk

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Professional OMC, transparent accounts, stable charges, no special levies expected |
| 7–8 | Functional OMC; charges reasonable; minor concerns at most |
| 5–6 | OMC somewhat opaque; charges rising; possible future levy for maintenance |
| 3–4 | OMC dysfunction reported; special levies possible; poor communication |
| 1–2 | Known remediation levy; dysfunctional OMC; legal disputes; major financial risk |

## 9. BER / Future Energy-Efficiency Risk

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | BER A1–A3; no upgrade needed; future-proof |
| 7–8 | BER B1–B3; good standard; minor improvement possible but not urgent |
| 5–6 | BER C1–C3; acceptable now but may need upgrade within 5–10 years |
| 3–4 | BER D; will likely need significant investment; regulatory risk |
| 1–2 | BER E–G; major energy efficiency problem; expensive to remediate; regulatory risk high |

## 10. Service-Charge Reasonableness

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | < €1,200/yr for standard apartment; well justified |
| 7–8 | €1,200–€2,000/yr; reasonable for location and amenities provided |
| 5–6 | €2,000–€2,800/yr; on the high side but explainable (lift, concierge, gym) |
| 3–4 | €2,800–€3,500/yr; expensive; eating significantly into yield |
| 1–2 | > €3,500/yr or rapidly escalating; unsustainable relative to rent |

## 11. Maintenance/Capex Risk

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Newly built/refurbished; everything modern; minimal capex for 10+ years |
| 7–8 | Good condition; one or two items may need attention within 5 years |
| 5–6 | Kitchen or bathroom aging; carpet/floors need refresh; €5–10k within 5 years |
| 3–4 | Significant refurb needed relatively soon; €10–20k exposure |
| 1–2 | Major works required immediately or imminently; €20k+ |

## 12. Layout and Size Quality

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Generous size for bed count; good layout; separate kitchen/living optional; storage |
| 7–8 | Good size; functional layout; meets tenant expectations |
| 5–6 | Adequate but compact; open-plan may limit appeal; minimal storage |
| 3–4 | Small for bed count; awkward layout; compromised living space |
| 1–2 | Very small; poor layout; difficult to furnish; below minimum expectations |

## 13. Parking Value

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Dedicated space in secure underground; adds €50–100/month rental premium |
| 7–8 | Dedicated space; secure; valuable in context |
| 5–6 | Shared or surface parking; some value but less premium |
| 3–4 | No parking but on-street available; minor penalty only |
| 1–2 | No parking in area where it matters significantly; tenant deterrent |

Note: In city-centre locations with excellent transport, lack of parking is less penalized (score 5–6 rather than 3–4).

## 14. Resale Liquidity

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Highly liquid; strong owner-occupier and investor demand; would sell quickly |
| 7–8 | Good liquidity; reasonable time to sell at fair value |
| 5–6 | Adequate but may take time; investor-only buyer pool |
| 3–4 | Narrow buyer pool; may need price concession to sell |
| 1–2 | Very illiquid; building issues or stigma make resale difficult |

## 15. Operational Hassle

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Set and forget; strong tenant pool, low maintenance, simple management |
| 7–8 | Low hassle; occasional attention needed |
| 5–6 | Moderate management load; tenancy turnover, some maintenance calls |
| 3–4 | Higher hassle; building issues, OMC disputes, or tenant management challenges |
| 1–2 | High hassle; ongoing problems likely; intensive management required |

## 16. Overall Risk-Adjusted Attractiveness

This is NOT a simple average. Weight the scores considering:
- How the property performs under stress
- Whether risks are diversifiable or existential
- The user's priority (resilience vs. yield)
- Whether unknowns are resolvable through diligence or inherent

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Exceptional risk-adjusted opportunity; would recommend confidently |
| 7–8 | Good investment; manageable risks; clear path to solid returns |
| 5–6 | Acceptable but with meaningful risks or modest returns |
| 3–4 | Marginal; risks may outweigh returns unless conditions improve |
| 1–2 | Poor risk-adjusted proposition; avoid unless price drops significantly |
